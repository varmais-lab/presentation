Why Early Response Matters
  • Motivational anchor: visible early results drive 90-day completion rates
  • Clinical validation: early glycemic improvement confirms protocol efficacy
  • Doctor confidence: early data gives referring physicians objective feedback
  • Relapse prevention: early wins reduce drop-off risk in weeks 3–6
Clinical Basis

Controlled carbohydrate restriction combined with structured meal timing has demonstrated consistent postprandial glucose improvement within 1–2 weeks in multiple structured dietary intervention studies. This intervention replicates those conditions through operationalized delivery.

Partnership Definition

Defined Role of the
Hospital Partner

13
🎓
Clinical Advisory
Named physician(s) contribute to intervention protocol design. Ensure clinical validity and appropriate patient selection criteria for each program cohort.
👤
Patient Referral
Referring suitable T2D and prediabetic patients through a structured, low-friction intake pathway. No additional clinical workload for the treating team.
🚨
Complex Case Oversight
Escalation of flagged participants (HbA1c spikes, medication changes, comorbidities) back to hospital partner for consultation and clinical decision-making.
✅
Outcome Validation
Review and co-sign structured outcome reports. Provides clinical credibility for program results and forms the foundation for research or publication collaboration.
⏱️

Minimal time commitment: The hospital partner role is designed to add clinical rigor without creating operational burden. Estimated physician time: 2–3 hours/month per active cohort.
