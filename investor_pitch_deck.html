Investor FAQs

The Three Brutal
Questions. Answered.

Q: Can this scale beyond one city?
Yes — by design. Cloud kitchen infrastructure is a proven playbook (Rebel Foods operates 450+ kitchens across 10+ countries). Our B2B corporate model means city expansion is triggered by a single enterprise contract, not consumer density. Kitchen economics are replicable. Clinical network is structured to operate independently per city with local advisor onboarding.
Q: Can this run without the founder?
Phase 1 is founder-led on GTM. That is appropriate for this stage. Phase 2 capital is allocated to hire city-level Sales Heads, a Clinical Head of Operations, and a Tech Product Lead. The protocol, kitchen ops, and clinical oversight layers are systemized and not founder-dependent. The institutional trust we are building through hospital partnerships outlasts any individual.
Q: Is this a tech company or an ops-heavy business?
Honestly? Phase 1 is ops-heavy. And that is the moat. The operational complexity of running clinical-grade food delivery with behavioral monitoring is exactly what makes this hard to copy. Phase 2 we layer tech: a proprietary participant app, a corporate dashboard, and the data platform. By Year 3 the data layer is the primary valuation driver. Ops-heavy entry → tech-multiplied scale.
