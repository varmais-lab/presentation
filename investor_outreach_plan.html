Outreach Message Templates

Angel / HNI — Doctor
LinkedIn / WhatsApp first message
"Dr. [Name], I'm building a structured metabolic health program for corporate employees in Hyderabad — specifically designed to solve what you see every day: patients who get the diagnosis, walk out of your clinic, and fall off within weeks.

We've built a compliance-controlled system — controlled meal delivery 6 days/week, weekly monitoring, clinical oversight — that operationalizes the lifestyle prescription.

We're raising a seed round and would love 20 minutes to get your perspective — both as a clinical expert and as someone who might want to be involved at the ground floor.

Would you be open to a brief call this week?"
✓ Leads with problem they know personally. ✓ Positions as seeking expertise, not money. ✓ Short. ✓ Clear ask.

The One Rule That Governs All 12 Weeks
Never
Approach a VC without at least one angel committed. Approach a strategic without a pilot running. Send a follow-up without a new development to share.
Always
Have a close date. Manufactured scarcity is real scarcity. Investors decide faster when the clock is running. Set it. Mean it. Enforce it.
The Unlock Sentence
"We have a 30-user pilot running, ₹[X]L committed, and we're closing this round by [date]. We have one slot remaining at this valuation."
Red Flags & Deal Killers
What to watch for — and how to respond — in every investor conversation.
🚫
Investor wants a board seat at seed
Any investor asking for board control at seed stage is a structural red flag. You need operational flexibility. Counter: offer a board observer seat as a compromise. If they insist, walk away.
🚫
Demands data rights as investment condition
Your anonymized cohort dataset is your Series A story. Any investor — especially strategic — who wants bulk data access as a condition is trying to extract your moat. Refuse cleanly: "Data governance is handled separately from investment terms."
🚫
Exclusivity clause from strategic investor
A hospital or diagnostic partner wanting city exclusivity sounds flattering but caps your expansion. If forced: time-limit to 6 months with automatic expiry, and cap geography tightly. Never sign open-ended exclusivity.
🚫
2× or higher liquidation preference
Standard is 1× non-participating. Any VC asking for 2× or participating preference is taking asymmetric downside protection at your expense. Push back firmly. It's a known negotiating tactic, not a standard term.
⚠️
Investor wants to "advise" operationally
Angels who want weekly check-ins or operational involvement slow you down. Offer a quarterly update call and a seat on a formal advisory board. Keep day-to-day decisions clean. Be friendly but firm about governance.
⚠️
Slow due diligence beyond 3 weeks
If a VC takes more than 3 weeks post-deck to make a decision at seed stage, they are not the right lead. Move on. Use them as a follow-on investor when your round closes. Their slowness tells you something about how they'll behave as a partner.
⚠️
Investor re-trades valuation mid-close
If someone agreed to ₹25 Cr and suddenly wants ₹18 Cr after due diligence with no new negative information, it's a negotiating tactic. Either push back with data ("Nothing has changed in the fundamentals") or walk. Re-traders are usually difficult partners.
⚠️
Strong anti-dilution at seed (full ratchet)
Full ratchet anti-dilution is extremely punishing if you raise a flat round later. Broad-based weighted average is standard and acceptable. Full ratchet is not. Push hard on this clause — most sophisticated investors won't insist.
ℹ️
VC wants to "lead but needs co-investor first"
This is chicken-and-egg positioning. They want social proof before committing. Your response: "We have committed angels in the round. What would you need to see to move to a term sheet?" Force specificity. Vague interest is not interest.
ℹ️
Strategic investor moves slowly, makes no decision
Corporates (hospitals, pharma) have complex internal approvals. If no movement in 4 weeks, they are not a seed investor for this round. Pivot to a commercial agreement (referral, diagnostic integration) and revisit investment at Series A.
ℹ️
Investor only interested in the data asset
If the conversation keeps returning to "what data will we have access to," this is a data extraction play, not a growth investment. Data rights in any investment agreement must be explicitly scoped, time-limited, and tied only to their pro-rata share of governance.
ℹ️
Investor compares you to Noom / Fitterfly unfavorably
Correct framing: "Noom is a behavioral app. Fitterfly is a coaching platform. We are the only company that delivers controlled nutrition. That's the compliance mechanism they don't have." You are not competing — you are in a different infrastructure category.
